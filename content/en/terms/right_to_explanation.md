## Definition

The right to explanation is a core component of algorithmic accountability, particularly within frameworks like the GDPR. It ensures that when an AI system makes a decision impacting a person's rights or opportunities, such as loan denial or hiring rejection, the individual can understand the logic behind it. This transparency helps prevent discrimination, allows for effective appeals, and builds trust in automated systems by demystifying 'black box' outcomes.

### Summary

A legal and ethical principle granting individuals the right to receive meaningful information about automated decisions that significantly affect them.
