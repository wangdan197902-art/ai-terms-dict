## Definition

In computational contexts, flux describes the rate of transfer of a quantity through a given area over time. In AI and data engineering, it often relates to data streaming, where information moves continuously from sources to processing units. Understanding flux is essential for managing real-time systems, ensuring that data pipelines can handle variable loads and maintain consistency during high-volume information transfers.

### Summary

Flux refers to the continuous flow or change of data, energy, or information within a system or network.
