## Definition

STIT stands for 'See To It That'. It is a branch of modal logic used primarily in philosophy and computer science to model agency and responsibility. It allows for the formal specification of what agents can bring about through their actions within a temporal structure. This logic is crucial for verifying multi-agent systems, ensuring that autonomous agents act according to specified obligations and constraints, thereby facilitating the design of ethical and accountable AI behaviors.

### Summary

STIT logic is a formal framework in deontic and temporal logic used to analyze agency, obligation, and intentional action.
