## Definition

Sam3 Video refers to the application of advanced segmentation models, potentially a hypothetical or specific version of Meta's Segment Anything Model, to video data. It involves tracking objects across frames, maintaining consistent masks over time, and handling occlusions and motion blur. This capability is essential for video editing, autonomous driving perception, and surveillance analysis, where dynamic object segmentation is required rather than static image processing.

### Summary

This term likely denotes video segmentation capabilities associated with a third-generation or specific variant of the Segment Anything Model applied to video streams.
