## Definition

Data-centric AI represents a paradigm shift in artificial intelligence development, focusing on systematically improving the data used to train models rather than solely optimizing algorithms or hyperparameters. Proponents argue that high-quality, well-labeled, and diverse datasets yield better performance gains than complex model tweaks. This methodology involves rigorous data auditing, labeling consistency checks, and iterative data refinement. By treating data as a first-class citizen, organizations can build more robust, fair, and accurate AI systems with less computational overhead.

### Summary

An approach to AI development that prioritizes improving the quality and quantity of training data over modifying model architectures.

## Key Concepts

- Data Quality
- Iterative Refinement
- Label Consistency
- Dataset Curation

## Use Cases

- Improving computer vision accuracy by fixing mislabeled images
- Reducing bias in hiring algorithms through balanced datasets
- Enhancing NLP model performance by correcting grammatical errors in training text
