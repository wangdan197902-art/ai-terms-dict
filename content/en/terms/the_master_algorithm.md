## Definition

Coined by Pedro Domingos in his book of the same name, the 'Master Algorithm' describes a theoretical unified framework for machine learning that could replicate all human learning processes. It envisions a single algorithm that can learn any concept given sufficient data, bridging different paradigms such as connectionism, symbolism, evolutionism, behaviorism, and analogizers. While currently speculative, it serves as a conceptual goal for researchers aiming to achieve general artificial intelligence through a comprehensive learning theory.

### Summary

The Master Algorithm refers to a hypothetical universal learning algorithm capable of deriving all knowledge from data.
