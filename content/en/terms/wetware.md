## Definition

Wetware originally referred to biological brain tissue but has evolved in cybernetics and transhumanism to describe the human mind or brain as a computational system. It contrasts with 'hardware' (physical machines) and 'software' (programs). In AI discussions, it may refer to bio-computing interfaces or the integration of neural tissue with digital systems. The term highlights the biological basis of cognition and intelligence, emphasizing the organic nature of human thought processes compared to silicon-based computation.

### Summary

A term referring to biological brains or living tissue, often used in contrast to hardware and software in computing contexts.
