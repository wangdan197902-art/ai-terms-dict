## Definition

This term encompasses the dual role of AI in democratic processes: enhancing efficiency through data analytics and posing risks via manipulation. On one hand, AI helps campaigns target voters and optimize messaging. On the other, it enables the creation of deepfakes, automated bot networks, and micro-targeted disinformation that can undermine election integrity. Regulatory bodies and tech companies are increasingly focusing on detecting AI-generated content and ensuring transparency to protect the fairness and trustworthiness of electoral outcomes.

### Summary

The application of AI technologies in electoral processes, including campaign management, voter analysis, and concerns over misinformation.

## Key Concepts

- Micro-targeting
- Disinformation
- Deepfakes
- Election Integrity

## Use Cases

- Analyzing voter sentiment through social media data
- Detecting fake news and bot activity online
- Automating administrative tasks in voting systems
