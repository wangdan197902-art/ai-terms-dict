## Definition

As generative AI produces increasing amounts of synthetic media, watermarking serves as a critical tool for transparency and accountability. It involves altering digital content—such as images, text, or audio—in ways that are imperceptible to humans but detectable by algorithms. This helps combat misinformation, copyright infringement, and deepfakes by allowing platforms and users to verify whether content was AI-generated. Techniques range from steganography in pixel values to statistical patterns in token selection for text generation.

### Summary

Watermarking in AI involves embedding invisible or visible identifiers into generated content to prove its origin and distinguish it from human-created material.
