## Definition

This domain focuses on creating hardware and software architectures that mimic the structure and function of the human brain. It encompasses artificial neural networks, neuromorphic chips, and cognitive computing systems. By leveraging principles of biological learning and memory, neurocomputing aims to solve complex problems such as pattern recognition, adaptive control, and intelligent decision-making more efficiently than traditional von Neumann architectures.

### Summary

Neurocomputing is an interdisciplinary field combining neuroscience, computer science, and engineering to develop computational models inspired by biological neural systems.

- Neuromorphic Engineering
- Biological Plausibility
- Parallel Processing
